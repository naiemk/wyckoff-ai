# Source Index

## Summary Of Resources Used

This skill was built from two primary source sets:

1. `The Wyckoff Methodology in Depth: How to Trade Financial Markets Logically` by Ruben Villahermosa Chaves, a 248-page Wyckoff text covering market structure, the three laws, phases, events, trading locations, and case studies.
2. The full `Wyckoff in the World of Cryptocurrency` archive page at Wyckoff Analytics, including all 14 paginated archive pages and 46 captured posts from `Wyckoff Crypto Report vol 14` through `Wyckoff Crypto Report 59`.

The book supplies the formal language of structure, laws, phases, events, and trading positions. The crypto archive supplies the applied adaptations needed for speculative, rotational, low-liquidity markets.

## Primary Book

### The Wyckoff Methodology in Depth

Use this source for the canonical framework:

- market cycle: accumulation -> markup -> distribution -> markdown
- trend assessment through speed, projection, depth, and channels
- the three laws: supply/demand, cause/effect, effort/result
- events: PS/PSY, SC/BC, AR, ST, spring/UTAD, SOS/SOW, LPS/LPSY
- phases A-E
- trading locations in Phase C, D, and E
- point-and-figure logic
- significant bar and reversal-of-movement concepts

Most valuable sections for scenario building:

- Chapters 1-5: market movement, trend health, and trading ranges
- Chapter 6: core structural variants
- Chapters 7-9: the three laws
- Chapters 10-13: accumulation / reaccumulation / distribution / redistribution
- Chapters 14-20: event taxonomy
- Chapters 21-25: phase logic
- Chapters 26-27: entry zones, significant bars, stops, and targets

## Specific Archive Ideas Worth Reusing

- Use spread charts to detect leadership, then execute on the corresponding USD or perp chart.
- A spring in crypto often needs confirmation through comparative strength, not just local price action.
- When Bitcoin rises aggressively, altcoins may consolidate; when Bitcoin pauses, altcoins may rotate higher.
- During global risk-off, institutions in crypto often hide first in Bitcoin and Tether rather than in low caps.
- A failed upthrust or failed short signal can be more informative than the original setup.
- Thematic indexes can reveal earlier leadership than single-asset charts.
