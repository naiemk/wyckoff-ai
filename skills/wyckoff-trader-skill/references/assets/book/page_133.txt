THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN THE SECONDARY  
TEST AND THE GENERIC TEST
Conceptually it is the same action: movement that develops to evaluate  the
commitment of traders in one direction and that must necessarily appe ar with
a decrease in the price ranges and volume to take it as valid.
The only difference is that the Secondary Test is a specific event  of
the Wyckoff methodology, with the connotations at a structural level alrea dy
commented; and the generic test is a global event, well known in the VS A
methodology (Volume Spread Analysis) that focuses primarily on the action
itself and what its result suggests to us.
