TREND  LINES
After identifying the nature of the trend, the next step is to build a gui deline
in order to take advantage of the movement. It is the simple connection
between two or more price points.
In a bearish trend, the trend line is drawn by connecting two
decreasing highs. This line is called the bid line because it is assumed that the
sellers will appear on it.
In an uptrend, the trendline is drawn by connecting two rising lows.
This line is called a demand line because it marks the point where  buyers are
supposed to appear.
We can continuously readjust the trend lines in order to adjust the one
that best suits the price action and therefore generated the most t ouches. The
more times the line has been respected, the stronger we will be a ble to
interpret it when it is played again in the future.
Note that a line with too much slope will be broken too soon, so it will
not be drawn correctly.
As long as the price remains within the established levels, it is  said
that the movement is healthy and it is appropriate to consider maintai ning or
adding positions.
