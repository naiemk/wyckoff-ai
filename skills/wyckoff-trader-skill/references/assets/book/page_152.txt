THE U PTHRUST  AFTER  D ISTRIBUTION  TEST
Although it can happen, a Secondary Test does not always appear after the
UTAD. This is due to the large amount of supply entering the market, whic h
causes the immediate bearish movement in the form of Sign of Weakness.
As with the Spring, it is generally better for the test to take pl ace. The
fact that the test does not appear may mean the loss of an opportunity, but
waiting for it to happen will help you avoid taking a possible bad short
position on an action that is actually a genuine bullish break (JAC/MSOS).
If there is a test, it should show less enthusiasm than the one see n
about UTAD. This is generally reflected by a halt in movement below t he
UTAD level and a reduction in price and volume ranges, which indicates a n
exhaustion of buyers and confirms the distribution scenario. On the ceiling of
this rise one can take sales positions.
If the test does not fall below the level that establishes the high of the
UTAD or the volume is higher, you should doubt the shock even if the price
is making lower highs. The most sensible thing is to wait for some addi tional
signal before selling (new shocks and successive successful tests).
