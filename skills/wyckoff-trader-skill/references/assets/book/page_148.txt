THE O RDINARY  S HAKEOUT
Spring and Terminal Shakeout are two similar events which occur during t he
development of an accumulation range. But there is another variant; the
Ordinary Shakeout, which is defined as a strong bearish push without
extensive prior preparation that occurs during the development of an uptrend
(reaccumulation). This is the main difference: the location.
The Ordinary Shakeout is characterized by wide price ranges and an
increase in volume. However, the volume can be high, medium or low.
