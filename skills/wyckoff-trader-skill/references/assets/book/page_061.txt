CHAPTER 9 - THE LAW OF EFFORT AND  
RESULT
In financial markets, effort is represented by volume while the res ult is
represented by price.
This means that the price action must reflect the volume action.
Without effort it can't have worked.
The aim is to assess the dominance of buyers or sellers through
convergence and divergence between price and volume.
THE IMPORTANCE OF VOLUME
Price is not the only important factor in financial markets. Perhaps even more
important is the character of the volume.
These two elements (price and volume) are part of the cornerstone of
the Wyckoff methodology.
Volume identifies the amount of stock (stocks, units, contracts) that
has changed hands. When large traders are interested in a security, thi s will
be reflected in the volume traded.
This is the first key concept: the participation of large traders is
identified by an increase in volume.
